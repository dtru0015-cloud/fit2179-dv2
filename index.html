Australia's Bushfire Crisis:
Are We Becoming More Vulnerable?

Exploring the trends, impacts, and growing risks of bushfires across Australia — from state-by-state exposure to the human cost of our most devastating seasons.

Bushfires are part of Australia's history.
But the scale and intensity of recent seasons —
and the number of people in harm's way —
are changing.


The data suggests we are becoming more vulnerable.

① Bushfire Risk Across Australia

Where does fire danger concentrate?

Australia's bushfire risk is not evenly distributed. New South Wales, Victoria, and the ACT consistently face the highest combined risk — driven by their dense vegetation, population pressure, and exposure to extreme fire weather.

② The Explanation

More fire. Hotter times.

Australia's warming trend is clear — and it aligns with increasingly severe fire seasons. As temperatures rise, conditions become more dangerous, and the fire season extends further into spring and autumn.

⑤ Fire Danger Throughout the Year

Idiom: Matrix Heatmap · Average FFDI · Source: Bureau of Meteorology

Victoria and NSW peak in January–February. The NT's fire season runs May–September — meaning Australia faces fire risk year-round.

⑥ Area Burned vs. Temperature Anomaly (2000–2024)

Idiom: Dual-Axis Line Chart · Sources: ABARES · Bureau of Meteorology (ACORN-SAT)

The 2019–20 Black Summer coincides with Australia's hottest year on record (+1.52°C above baseline). Warmer conditions align with larger burned areas.

③ The Human Cost

The damage is personal.

Behind the hectares and indices are real losses — lives ended, homes destroyed, and communities uprooted.

⑧ Deaths and Homes Destroyed — Major Events

Idiom: Connected Dot Plot · Sources: CFA, NSW RFS, QFES, etc.

Black Saturday (2009) remains the deadliest single event with 173 lives lost. The Black Summer caused the most property destruction — 3,000+ homes.

⑨ Economic Losses Are Escalating

Idiom: Bubble Scatter Plot · Source: Insurance Council of Australia

The Black Summer's $103B economic loss dwarfs all previous events combined. Bubble size represents direct deaths.

④ The Future

More Australians may be in harm's way.

As Australia's population grows and spreads into fire-prone areas, the number of people exposed to high fire risk is increasing — even as fire seasons grow more intense.

⑩ Population Exposed to High Bushfire Risk

Idiom: Dot Matrix / Bubble Plot · Sources: ABS (2001–2021) & Geoscience Australia

NSW's exposed population grew from 1.24M in 2001 to 1.85M in 2021 — a 49% increase. Every state shows upward growth.

⑪ Risk is Spreading: Change in Fire Risk Score (2001–2021)

Idiom: Slope Chart · Source: Geoscience Australia

Every state has seen its fire risk score increase over 20 years. NSW shows the largest rise (+0.23), while even lower-risk states like WA and SA trend upward.

The Bottom Line

🔥 Extreme fire seasons are becoming more frequent — three of the four worst seasons occurred after 2019.
🌡️ Conditions are getting hotter — 2019 was Australia's warmest year on record, with 8 of the 9 hottest years since 2013.
👥 More Australians are living in harm's way — exposure to high fire risk zones has grown in every state since 2001.

The data is clear: we are becoming more vulnerable.

Bushfires are not new to Australia. But the combination of extreme fire weather, repeated severe seasons, and growing human exposure suggests our vulnerability is increasing.
